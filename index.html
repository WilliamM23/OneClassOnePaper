The Florida Aquifer

What is it?

The Florida Aquifer has an area of approximately 100,000 square miles and is one of the most productive aquifers in the world. It is around 1,000 feet thick and can store fresh water nearly 2,000 feet below the surface. The Florida Aquifer is essential as the main water source for 90% of people in northeast and central Florida.

Natural Springs

When the aquifer overflows and its water hits the land surface, a natural spring is formed. This natural process results in a variety of beautiful Florida springs that can have clear to red iron color colors due to minerals around it.

Viability

On average, Florida receives about 51 inches of rainfall a year. Due to evaporation, only about 13 inches annually enter and recharge the aquifer.

Water Depletion

The Florida Department of Environmental Protection states that the current water supply will NOT meet future demand.

    Help Preserve the Floridan Aquifer System

  • Reduce water consumption
  • Use Florida-friendly landscaping
  • Check your irrigation system
  • Use fertilizer sparingly
  • Have septic tanks inspected every 2-3 years
  • Dispose of hazardous household chemicals at an approved landfill
